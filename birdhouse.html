INTERACTIVE BIRDHOUSE + FEEDER 2 in 1

INTRO

An interactive birdhouse that promotes a bird-friendly community through collective public effort. Instead of leaving wildlife protection solely to environmental organizations, this design invites everyone to participate in supporting bird populations. With a simple tap on the birdhouse, users can provide food for local birds, making conservation accessible and engaging for all. This small daily action creates meaningful impact, turning casual interactions into lasting habits while fostering a deeper connection between humans and birds.

INFO

Role
Product Designer

PROJECT BACKGROUND

Research indicates that the decline of bird populate has resulted in numerous enviromental issues. An idea came to my mind: Is there a solution to this issue that everyone can participate?

PROBLEM STATEMENT

"How can we, as citizens, effectively address the declining bird populations in urban areas while promoting coexistence between birds and people?

INITIAL IDEAS & THUMBNAIL SKETCHES

My research phase involved analyzing existing bird-friendly designs to identify strengths and limitations.

My initial sketches explored various forms, from cubic structures to egg-shaped designs. My first concept focused on the satisfying tactile experience of pressing a mechanical switch, connecting that sensation to the act of helping birds. As I refined the design, I transformed from square to circular shapes to deter predators like squirrels and rats, and incorporated hanging mechanisms.
