FLEETFLOW E-SCOOTERS

INTRO

This project proposes a transformative approach to shared scooter fleet management through the design and implementation of an Autonomous Scooter Collection Vehicle (ACV)—a compact, self-driving vehicle specifically engineered to revolutionize how shared mobility fleets are maintained and distributed. Rather than reactive, labor-dependent collection, this system introduces proactive, data-driven fleet optimization that continuously adapts to real-time urban mobility patterns.

CURRENT PROBLEM

The Escooter industry faces a fundamental paradox: while demand for micro-mobility solutions continues to grow, the operational model remains economically unsustainable and logistically inefficient. Current systems rely heavily on human labor—"juicers" and "chargers"—who manually collect, charge, and redistribute scooters throughout cities during nighttime hours. This labor-intensive approach consumes 30-40% of operational revenue, with major operators like Lime and Bird spending between $3-8 per scooter per collection cycle in labor costs.
