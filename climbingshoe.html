VIPER BOULDERING SHOES

INTRO

This conceptual bouldering shoe represents my exploration of functional form within climbing footwear design. Through this project, I experimented with the relationship between ergonomics and aesthetics in technical athletic equipment. The design process began by studying the my own experience of climbing, leading to a downturned silhouette with an asymmetric last that follows the natural curl of the foot during edging and hooking movements.

PRODUCT DETAILS

A key exploration in this project was the repositioning and the tensioning system of the shoeslace. I designed a micro-adjustable dial of tightening up the shoelace instead of the traditional method. You can tight up the shoelace by turning the dial. Additionally, instead of placing the shoelace centrally, I shifted it to the lateral side of the shoe to eliminate pressure points when the climber's foot is pressed against the wall.
